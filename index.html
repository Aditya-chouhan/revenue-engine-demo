The CRM system behind the data

Full file: crm-system-design.md — the schema, routing logic, and automation rules a production CRM would need to produce this dataset.

stageexit criteriaSLA
LeadSignal score ≥ 3.0Scored within 1 hour (automated)
MQLRep confirms budget/authority/need/timingActioned within 6 business hours
SQLDiscovery call completed, deal shape documentedCall booked within 3 business days
OpportunityProspect returns Won/Lost decisionFollow-up every 5 business days
Closed Won/LostHandoff to CS / lost_reason loggedHandoff within 24 hours

Lead routing is a weighted round-robin with a signal-score fast lane: leads scoring ≥8.0 skip the queue and route to whoever has the lowest open-SQL load, because a distress signal decays — the marginal cost of queueing is a lost deal, not a slower one. The load-balance check: rep-level lead counts land within 3% of an even three-way split (710 / 639 / 731), confirming the rule works as designed.

What's not built (named, not hidden)

Stale-lead decay is designed in crm-system-design.md but not simulated in the generator yet. Category-specialized routing is designed but deliberately not activated — there isn't enough per-rep, per-category sample size to trust the win-rate difference isn't noise, and shipping an unvalidated segmentation rule would be worse than not having one. CAC and retention aren't modeled because there's no spend or renewal-event data in this dataset to compute them honestly — adding a synthetic spend log and a renewal event would make both a straightforward next step.
